Stan Peceny

I am a PhD candidate in Georgia Tech's School of Cybersecurity and Privacy. My research focuses on cryptography and security. I have traveled to over 70 countries and hold a PADI Rescue Diver license.

  • About Me

Education and Work Experience

  • 2025 Ph.D. Computer Science

    GEORGIA INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY

    I have a broad interest in all areas of cryptography, with my PhD research specifically focused on secure multi-party computation (MPC). I have authored multiple papers in this area, all with the aim of making MPC more concretely efficient. My research advisor is Prof. Vlad Kolesnikov.

    In addition to my research at Georgia Tech, I have completed two research internships with Visa’s Advanced Cryptography team, mentored by Dr. Peter Rindal and Prof. Srinivasan Raghuraman (Palo Alto, Foster City); three research internships with Google’s Private Computing team, mentored by Prof. Mariana Raykova and Dr. Karn Seth (New York); and one research internship with Inpher, a start-up specializing in MPC, mentored by Dr. Mariya Georgieva (New York).
  • From 2017 to 2019, I worked as a software engineer at Inpher in New York, under the mentorship of Prof. Dimitar Jetchev (EPFL) and Prof. Nicolas Gama (University of Versailles).
